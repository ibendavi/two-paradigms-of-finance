Key Finding

Archibald Hutcheson's 1720 pamphlets contain the earliest known systematic equity valuation exercises, computing "intrinsick value" of South Sea Company stock using a net asset value (NAV) approach â€” assets (government annuities valued using standard bond math) minus liabilities, divided by shares outstanding. This is balance-sheet valuation, not earnings capitalization or discounted cash flow.

Details

Valuation Method: Net Asset Value (NAV)

Hutcheson values equity from the balance sheet side â€” total assets minus liabilities, divided by shares outstanding. This is NOT earnings-based (V=E/r) or dividend-based (DDM), but rather a fundamental/intrinsic value approach.

  1. Assets: Value of government annuities held by the Company (present value of annuity streams at various discount rates)
  2. Liabilities: Amounts owed by the Company on various obligations

The "Intrinsick Value" Concept

Hutcheson explicitly uses the term "intrinsick value" to mean fundamental value based on assets and income, as distinct from market price. He argues the market price has completely detached from fundamentals. This is the same concept Graham & Dodd would later call "intrinsic value" in 1934 â€” but 214 years earlier.

Sophistication Level

  • Compound interest / present value calculations: Yes
  • Discount rate sensitivity: Yes (shows values at 3%, 4%, 5%, 6%)
  • Dilution from new issuances: Yes
  • Sustainability of dividends: Yes
  • Trading/operating income projection: Basic (rough estimate of trade profits)
  • Earnings capitalization (V=E/r): No â€” uses asset-based approach, not earnings multiple
  • Risk adjustment: No â€” uses risk-free government rates
  • Market efficiency concept: Implicit â€” argues market is wrong, intrinsic value differs from price

Significance

  1. Earliest systematic equity valuation: Predates all known formal equity valuation exercises. The next comparable work is over a century later.
  2. Practitioner, not academic: This is real-time financial analysis by a Member of Parliament trying to warn investors â€” pure practitioner stream.
  3. The "intrinsick value" vs. market price gap: Hutcheson shows the stock is 3-5x overvalued. This is the earliest known use of fundamental valuation to identify a bubble â€” foreshadowing Graham & Dodd by two centuries.
  4. NAV approach dominates early finance: The earnings-based approach (capitalize earnings, discount dividends) would take another century+ to develop. Early equity valuation was balance-sheet-driven.
  5. Bond math applied to equity's underlying assets: Hutcheson uses present value of annuities (known since de Witt 1671) to value the Company's assets, then derives equity value residually. This is NOT the same as discounting equity cash flows (Williams 1938) â€” it is balance-sheet valuation where the PV calculation applies to the debt/annuity assets, not to the equity itself.
