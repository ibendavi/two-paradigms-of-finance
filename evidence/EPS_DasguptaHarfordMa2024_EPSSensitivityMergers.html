Dasgupta, Harford & Ma (2024): EPS Sensitivity and Mergers

Dasgupta, Sudipto, Jarrad Harford, and Fangyuan Ma. "EPS Sensitivity and Mergers." *Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis* 59, no. 2 (March 2024): 521-556. (Dasgupta at Chinese University of Hong Kong; Harford at University of Washington; Ma at Peking University.)

Key Finding

Dasgupta, Harford & Ma's 2024 Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis paper provides the most comprehensive evidence that EPS accretion/dilution concerns shape M&A deal structure, premiums, and acquirer behavior. Using 3,799 mergers from 1991-2017, they construct a novel measure ‚Äî S_ŒîEPS_AS, the standardized change in acquirer EPS if the deal were paid entirely in stock ‚Äî and employ a regression discontinuity design (RDD) at the zero threshold. The key result: the probability of paying cash jumps by 5-17 percentage points at the exact threshold where an all-stock deal would become EPS-dilutive (DILUTIVE_AS coefficient = 0.130***, t = 5.02). 71.4% of completed deals would be dilutive if done entirely in stock. For mixed deals, the cash-stock mix is carefully calibrated so that the intended EPS (S_ŒîEPS_INT) stays just above zero ‚Äî managers optimally choose the payment fraction to achieve EPS accretion. Deals are structured to avoid shareholder voting when dilutive (keeping stock issuance below the 20% threshold). The market rewards accretive stock deals with higher announcement CARs (ACCRETIVE coefficient = 0.020***, 3-day window), but this advantage vanishes by deal completion. EPS sensitivity creates real distortions: acquirers of if-stock-dilutive deals finance cash payments by issuing debt and pre-deal cash hoarding, and they cut capital expenditures and R&D prior to deals. Among "high-buys-low" stock deals (high P/E acquirer buying low P/E target), there is abnormal clustering of deals that are barely accretive, with accretive deals associated with significantly lower premiums (-0.130*** to -0.180***). The effect is STRONGER after 2001, when the abolition of pooling accounting (SFAS 141/142) made the purchase method mandatory, increasing EPS sensitivity for stock deals. For the EPS project, this paper extends Andrade (1997) and Garvey, Milbourne & Xie (2013) to show that EPS concerns pervade every dimension of M&A: payment method, deal structure, premium negotiation, shareholder voting, pre-deal financial policy, and post-announcement market reaction.

Details

Section I: Introduction (pp. 521-527)

The motivation: Practitioner focus on EPS accretion in M&A is pervasive ‚Äî a Factiva search yields 309,505 results for "mergers" combined with "EPS accretion" or "EPS dilution." Yet only 20% of deals have synergy forecasts, and only 2% provide NPV numbers. EPS accretion is the metric practitioners actually use.

Novel contribution: While Andrade (1997) studied announcement returns and Garvey et al. (2013) studied deal propensity, Dasgupta et al. study HOW deals are STRUCTURED to achieve EPS accretion ‚Äî the payment method, the cash-stock mix, the premium, and the associated financial policy distortions.

Key innovation ‚Äî S_ŒîEPS_AS: The standardized all-stock change in EPS = (combined EPS under all-stock - acquirer's pre-deal EPS) / acquirer's stock price. This measures what would happen to the acquirer's EPS if the ENTIRE deal were paid in stock, regardless of how it is actually paid.

DILUTIVE_AS: Indicator = 1 if S_ŒîEPS_AS < 0 (all-stock deal would be EPS-dilutive).

4.E Shareholder voting:

Deals are structured to avoid triggering the 20% stock issuance threshold (which requires shareholder vote) WHEN the deal would be dilutive. For dilutive deals, the fraction of mixed deals is 5% higher when an all-stock deal would cross the 20% threshold. No such effect for accretive deals. Dilutive stock deals also face higher deal failure rates (11.8% vs. 9.9%).

Key finding: Accretive stock deals earn significantly higher announcement CARs (~2% in 3-day window), but this advantage VANISHES by the time of deal completion. The market initially rewards EPS accretion but the effect is short-lived.

"Investors form more favorable perceptions about the accretive stock deals at least in the short window around deal announcements... the return difference vanishes by the time of deal completion."

The authors note this suggests the "mechanical combined EPS measure mainly captures the cosmetic EPS impact and has little to do with deal fundamentals."

Section IV.G: Distortions to Financial and Investment Policies (pp. 544-545)

Table 6 ‚Äî Total shareholder gains and cash payment for if-stock-dilutive deals:

EPS-driven cash payments are financed through:

  1. Issuing debt ‚Äî Cash payments mainly financed by debt issuance
  2. Preserving financial flexibility ‚Äî Acquirers save cash prior to the deal
  3. Cutting investment ‚Äî Managers reduce capital expenditures and R&D before deals

These effects are found ONLY for "if-stock-dilutive" deals ‚Äî deals that would have been dilutive if paid in stock. Cash is used to avoid dilution, but the cash must come from somewhere.

Combined CARs: DILUTIVE_AS √ó CASH% interaction is significantly negative (-0.074** to -0.079*). Deals structured to mitigate dilution via cash payment are associated with LOWER total shareholder gains. The EPS-friendly structure destroys value.

"Deals structured to alleviate the acquirer's EPS dilution are not associated with superior shareholder gains in the long run, although the EPS-friendly structure helps with gaining shareholder support in the shorter term following deal announcement."

EPS sensitivity is STRONGER after 2001. This coincides with the abolition of pooling accounting (SFAS 141/142 in June 2001). Under pooling, stock deals could be EPS-friendly because the target's book value (not market value) was used. Under the purchase method (mandatory post-2001), goodwill and fair value adjustments made stock deals more EPS-costly. The elimination of the EPS-friendly accounting method INCREASED EPS sensitivity.

5.C Accounting rules:

The pooling method was "much more EPS-friendly" for stock deals because book values (not fair values) were combined and there was no goodwill amortization. After SFAS 141/142 abolished pooling, "methods of payment are even more sensitive to if-stock dilution after goodwill amortization was essentially abolished." The accounting rule change created MORE EPS sensitivity, not less ‚Äî confirming that EPS concerns drive deal structure, and accounting rules mediate the relationship.

Section VI: Conclusion (pp. 551)

"We argue that in the absence of hard information about deal synergy, the EPS impact of a deal is an easy-to-communicate metric that managers can use to convince shareholders about the merits of a deal. As a result, focus on EPS has become part of M&A practice."

"Our results do not imply that EPS accretion is the primary driver of mergers; indeed, many dilutive deals are proposed and completed. Rather, we show that while efficiency would require that the NPV of the acquisition... should be the only consideration in determining which deals get done, the EPS impact of the deal is also an important factor and this factor distorts merger decisions."

Significance

1. The Most Comprehensive Evidence on EPS in M&A

This paper extends Andrade (1997) from market reactions to deal STRUCTURE and extends Garvey et al. (2013) from deal propensity to payment method, premiums, and financial policy. Every dimension of M&A is affected by EPS concerns: what you pay (cash vs. stock), how much you pay (premium), how you finance cash (debt, cut investment), whether shareholders vote, and what the market thinks.

2. The RDD Provides Clean Identification

The sharp discontinuity at S_ŒîEPS_AS = 0 in Figure 2 is the M&A equivalent of Almeida et al.'s (2016) repurchase discontinuity. Investment opportunities, synergies, and other deal characteristics are continuous at zero; only EPS dilution status is discontinuous. The 5-17 pp cash fraction jump is identified by EPS concerns alone.

3. 71.4% of Deals Would Be Dilutive ‚Äî EPS Binds for Most Deals

The finding that nearly three-quarters of deals would be EPS-dilutive if paid entirely in stock means EPS concerns are NOT marginal ‚Äî they affect the MAJORITY of deals. This explains why cash has become the dominant payment method and why practitioners obsess over accretion/dilution.

4. Mixed Deals Are Precisely Calibrated for EPS

Figure 3 is stunning: for mixed deals, the cash-stock mix is chosen so that S_ŒîEPS_INT sits JUST above zero. This is penny-level EPS management in M&A ‚Äî the exact analog of Hribar et al.'s (2006) finding that 84.1% of accretive repurchases change EPS by $0.01-$0.02. Whether repurchasing or acquiring, managers calibrate to the EPS threshold.

5. Accretive Deals Pay Lower Premiums ‚Äî Targets Subsidize Accretion

The finding that accretive stock deals have premiums 13-18 pp lower and target CARs 9-12 pp lower means that EPS accretion comes at the target's expense. High P/E acquirers can use the "accretive" label to justify paying less. This is the EPS paradigm's M&A prediction: accretion is treated as value, even when it is purely mechanical.

6. EPS-Driven Cash Destroys Value

The negative interaction (DILUTIVE_AS √ó CASH%) in Table 6 shows that deals structured to avoid dilution via cash payment produce LOWER combined shareholder gains. The EPS-friendly structure is NPV-unfriendly ‚Äî exactly the distortion the EPS paradigm predicts when managers maximize EPS rather than value.

7. Pre-Deal Investment Cuts Confirm the Capital Budgeting Distortion

Managers cut capex and R&D before deals to preserve cash for EPS-friendly payment. This connects directly to Almeida et al. (2016) and to the capital budgeting paper: EPS concerns distort not just payout but also real investment, even before the deal is announced.

8. The Pooling-to-Purchase Transition Is a Natural Experiment

The abolition of pooling accounting in 2001 made stock deals more EPS-costly, increasing EPS sensitivity of payment method. This is powerful evidence that ACCOUNTING RULES drive deal structure through the EPS channel. A change in reporting rules ‚Äî with no change in economic fundamentals ‚Äî changed how managers structure deals. This confirms that reported EPS, not economic value, is the operative metric.

9. Market Reaction: Short-Term Reward, Long-Term Irrelevance

The 2% announcement CAR for accretive deals that vanishes by completion suggests the market initially rewards EPS accretion but eventually recognizes its cosmetic nature. This parallels Andrade's (1997) finding of a coefficient of ~0.3 (partial capitalization) and Hribar et al.'s (2006) discounted ERC on repurchase-induced EPS.

10. The Factiva Evidence Quantifies Practitioner Obsession

309,505 search results for "mergers" + "EPS accretion/dilution" vs. only 20% of deals with synergy forecasts and 2% with NPV numbers. The ratio of EPS-to-NPV in practitioner discourse is approximately 100:1. This is the quantitative evidence that practitioners operate in the EPS paradigm, not the NPV paradigm.

Connections

  • Andrade (1997) ‚Äî "Do Appearances Matter?" The precursor. Andrade shows EPS accretion predicts announcement returns (coefficient ~0.3). Dasgupta et al. extend to show EPS concerns drive deal STRUCTURE ‚Äî payment method, premium, financing ‚Äî not just market reaction.
  • Garvey, Milbourne & Xie (2013) ‚Äî "What Will It Do for My EPS?" Show EPS accretion predicts which firms become targets. Dasgupta et al. extend from target selection to deal structure.
  • Shleifer & Vishny (2003) ‚Äî "Stock Market Driven Acquisitions." Propose overvalued acquirers use stock. Dasgupta et al. distinguish EPS sensitivity from misvaluation: EPS sensitivity is stronger after 2001, while the misvaluation channel would predict the opposite (more overvaluation-driven deals pre-2001). The two channels are "not mutually exclusive" but empirically separable.
  • Hribar, Jenkins & Johnson (2006) ‚Äî "Stock Repurchases as an Earnings Management Device." Both papers show penny-level EPS management ‚Äî repurchases calibrated to $0.01-$0.02 in Hribar et al., deal structures calibrated to just-above-zero accretion in Dasgupta et al.
  • Almeida, Fos & Kronlund (2016) ‚Äî "The Real Effects of Share Repurchases." Both papers show EPS-motivated capital allocation distorts investment. Almeida et al. show post-repurchase investment cuts; Dasgupta et al. show pre-deal investment cuts to finance cash payments.
  • Huang, Marquardt & Zhang (2014) ‚Äî "Why Do Managers Avoid EPS Dilution?" Extend the same logic from financing choice (debt vs. equity) to M&A payment choice (cash vs. stock). Both use the EPS dilution condition; both show managers prefer the EPS-friendly option.
  • Bens, Nagar, Skinner & Wong (2003) ‚Äî "Employee Stock Options, EPS Dilution, and Stock Repurchases." Bens et al. show EPS dilution management via repurchases; Dasgupta et al. show EPS dilution management via M&A payment method. Both involve managing the denominator (shares outstanding).
  • Lys & Vincent (1995) ‚Äî "An Analysis of Value Destruction in AT&T's Acquisition of NCR." The single case study; Dasgupta et al. provide the large-sample evidence.
  • Ben-David & Chinco (EPS) ‚Äî Dasgupta et al. demonstrate that the EPS paradigm operates across ALL dimensions of M&A. The S_ŒîEPS_AS measure IS the EPS paradigm's deal evaluation criterion: does the deal boost or dilute the acquirer's EPS? The finding that 71.4% of deals would be dilutive in stock explains the dominance of cash in modern M&A.
  • Ben-David & Chinco (EPS x PE) ‚Äî The relative P/E analysis (Table 7, Figure A1) directly connects to the EPS x P/E pricing model. High P/E acquirers can buy low P/E targets with stock AND be accretive ‚Äî this is the "bootstrap game" (high P/E √ó target E = EPS boost), quantified across 3,319 deals.
  • Ben-David & Chinco (maxEPS paradigm) ‚Äî The Factiva evidence (309,505 results for EPS accretion vs. 2% of deals with NPV numbers) quantifies the dominance of the EPS paradigm in M&A practice. The historical shift from pooling to purchase accounting (2001) provides a dated structural break in the EPS-sensitivity of M&A.
  • Ben-David & Chinco (EPS and capital budgeting) ‚Äî Pre-deal investment cuts to finance EPS-friendly cash payments are direct evidence that EPS maximization distorts capital budgeting. Managers forego investment to preserve cash for EPS-accretive deal structures.
  • Ben-David & Chinco, Meli (EPS and financial markets) ‚Äî When interest rates fall, the cost of financing cash payments declines, making cash deals more EPS-friendly. This may contribute to the secular increase in cash-financed M&A documented alongside the bond boom and leverage increase.
